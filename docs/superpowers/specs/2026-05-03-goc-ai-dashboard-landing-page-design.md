# GOC AI Dashboard Landing Page Design Framework

## 1. Background

GOC AI Dashboard is an overseas advertising attribution product for advertisers and growth teams. The landing page will support the SaaS go-to-market motion and prepare a clear framework for later copy, screenshots, customer proof, and product documentation.

The current design goal is not to write final marketing copy. It is to establish the landing page structure, narrative logic, and content placeholders so later product documents can be uploaded and filled into the right sections.

## 2. Product Positioning

### One-line Summary

GOC AI Dashboard redefines cross-platform attribution logic, provides a unified measurement standard, and helps advertisers answer the core growth question: how should ad performance be optimized?

### Core Problems Solved

1. Decision-grade cross-platform attribution
   - Advertising platforms use different attribution rules.
   - Each platform tends to credit its own channel.
   - Cross-platform performance becomes fragmented.
   - Growth teams cannot compare channel contribution under one consistent logic.

2. Upper-funnel channel measurement
   - Upper-funnel and mid-funnel channels are often undervalued by short-window attribution.
   - GOC uses cross-device, long-window attribution and user conversion cycle analysis to estimate the contribution of these channels under a unified framework.

### Primary Audience

The first priority audience is overseas advertisers and growth teams. They care about:

- Whether attribution data is trustworthy.
- Which channels contribute to conversions under a consistent standard.
- How to evaluate upper-funnel impact.
- How to optimize budget allocation.
- Whether the system can be tried with low friction.

### Conversion Goal

The primary CTA is free trial or account creation. Secondary CTAs can include product walkthrough, demo request, or contact sales, but the page should be optimized around trial intent.

## 3. Recommended Narrative Direction

Use a problem-diagnosis landing page structure.

The page should not begin as a feature catalog. It should first name the attribution problem advertisers already feel:

> Every ad platform claims credit, but your budget needs one consistent measurement standard.

Then GOC is introduced as the system that provides one unified cross-platform attribution standard, helping advertisers compare channel contribution, measure upper-funnel impact, and optimize budget decisions.

This direction is preferred because:

- It starts from the buyer's pain, not the product's internal feature list.
- It fits the audience of advertisers and growth teams.
- It makes the product's unified measurement logic easier to understand.
- It supports a free trial CTA because the user is invited to test whether GOC can clarify their own channel performance.

## 5. Page Information Architecture

### Section 1: Hero

Purpose:

- Explain GOC's value in the first screen.
- Establish the idea of unified cross-platform attribution.
- Drive free trial intent.

Recommended message:

- Every advertising platform measures performance differently and tends to claim its own contribution.
- GOC gives advertisers one consistent cross-platform attribution standard.
- The result is clearer channel comparison, better upper-funnel evaluation, and more confident budget optimization.

Content placeholders:

- Final product English name.
- Primary CTA URL or trial flow.
- Secondary CTA choice: product walkthrough, demo, or contact sales.
- Trial microcopy, such as setup support, credit card requirement, and expected time to first attribution view.
- Optional dashboard screenshot or product animation.

### Section 2: Problem Diagnosis

Purpose:

- Create immediate pain recognition.
- Show why platform-reported attribution cannot be used as the only budget decision basis.

Key points:

- Different platforms use different attribution windows and models.
- Platforms naturally tend to claim conversions for themselves.
- Cross-platform results are fragmented and hard to compare.
- Growth teams may over-invest in channels that look good only under platform-native attribution.

Suggested angle:

> Meta, Google, TikTok, and other platforms can all claim contribution to the same conversion. GOC helps advertisers rebuild the attribution view from a neutral, unified standard.

Content placeholders:

- Typical attribution conflict examples.
- Supported ad platforms.
- Before/after narrative showing fragmented attribution vs unified attribution.

### Section 3: GOC Method

Purpose:

- Explain the underlying method without becoming too technical.
- Make GOC feel like a measurement framework, not just a dashboard.

Core message:

- Collect campaign and conversion signals across paid channels.
- Normalize platform-reported data into one consistent measurement framework.
- Rebuild attribution across devices, windows, and user conversion cycles.
- Translate attribution results into channel comparison and budget optimization guidance.

Method boundary:

GOC should not be positioned as simply merging platform-reported numbers. It rebuilds attribution using unified rules across channels, devices, and conversion windows. The page should avoid implying that any attribution model can prove a single absolute causal truth without assumptions.

Content placeholders:

- Attribution methodology name, if available.
- Supported attribution windows.
- Supported devices and identity signals.
- Supported event types, such as install, signup, purchase, subscription, lead, or custom conversion.

### Section 4: Three Capability Pillars

Purpose:

- Translate the product's value into three clear capability groups.

Pillar A: Decision-Grade Attribution

- Estimate channel contribution under one unified logic.
- Reduce self-reported platform bias.
- Compare channels under one standard.

Pillar B: Upper-Funnel Channel Measurement

- Measure brand, awareness, content, and consideration channels.
- Use cross-device and long-window attribution.
- Connect early influence to later conversion.

Pillar C: Performance Optimization

- Turn attribution insights into budget decisions.
- Identify under-credited channels.
- Support budget reallocation and campaign optimization.

Content placeholders:

- Feature screenshots for each pillar.
- Metrics shown in each module.
- Reports or recommendations users receive.

### Section 5: Dashboard Preview

Purpose:

- Make the product tangible.
- Show that the unified attribution logic becomes a usable operating dashboard.

Recommended visuals:

- Channel contribution comparison.
- User conversion journey.
- Cross-device attribution path.
- Conversion cycle timeline.
- Budget optimization recommendation.

Content placeholders:

- Dashboard screenshots or prototype.
- Three most important charts to show.
- Whether screenshots need customer data masking.

### Section 6: Trusted Data Setup

Purpose:

- Reduce trial friction by explaining what data is needed and why advertisers can trust the setup.
- Address privacy, masking, and platform connection concerns before asking for trial conversion.

Key points:

- Supported ad platforms and data sources.
- Supported conversion and event data.
- Privacy, data masking, and security posture.
- How GOC avoids relying only on platform-native attribution claims.
- What is required to start a trial.

Content placeholders:

- Platform connection list.
- Event integration requirements.
- Data handling and masking rules.
- Security or compliance statements.
- Trial setup checklist.

### Section 7: Proof

Purpose:

- Build confidence that the product is already used in real scenarios.
- Reduce trial hesitation.

Recommended proof types:

- Customer logos.
- Industry examples.
- Attribution conflict resolved.
- Budget optimization result.
- Trial outcome metrics.

Content placeholders:

- Public customer names or anonymized customer profiles.
- Case study metrics.
- Supported industries.
- Existing customer quotes.

### Section 8: Final CTA

Purpose:

- Convert interest into free trial.
- Make trial value explicit.

Recommended message:

> Start measuring global ads with one attribution standard.

CTA:

> Start Free Trial

Supporting copy:

- Tell users what they can see during trial.
- Mention onboarding support if available.
- Clarify whether credit card is required.

Content placeholders:

- Trial duration.
- Trial requirements.
- Setup support.
- Expected time to first attribution insight.

## 6. Tone and Style

The tone should be confident, analytical, and global-facing. It should avoid sounding like a cheap alternative to existing MMPs.

Use language that emphasizes:

- Unified measurement.
- Neutral attribution.
- Consistent cross-platform standards.
- Upper-funnel value.
- Budget optimization.
- Global growth.

Avoid overemphasizing:

- Cheap pricing as the main claim.
- Overly technical attribution jargon before user pain is established.
- Direct competitor attacks without evidence.
- Absolute claims such as proving the only truth, perfect attribution, or exact causal contribution unless backed by methodology and evidence.

## 7. Suggested Visual Direction

The page should feel like a serious B2B SaaS analytics product:

- Clean dashboard-led visual system.
- Dark or deep navy hero option for data credibility.
- Accent colors for channel comparison and attribution paths.
- Charts, journey lines, and platform logos used to communicate cross-platform complexity.
- Clear CTA contrast for free trial conversion.

The page does not need heavy decoration. The core visual anchor should be multiple platform-reported attribution paths converging into one unified GOC attribution view. This is more distinctive than a generic analytics dashboard and reinforces the product's core promise.

## 8. Materials Needed Later

When the user uploads follow-up documents, sort the information into these buckets:

1. Product facts
   - Official product name.
   - Supported ad platforms.
   - Supported conversion events.
   - Attribution windows.
   - Device and identity logic.
   - Data connection and security requirements.

2. Product visuals
   - Dashboard screenshots.
   - Attribution journey screenshots.
   - Reports and charts.
   - Demo video or animation ideas.

3. Customer proof
   - Customer logos.
   - Case studies.
   - Performance metrics.
   - Testimonials.

4. Trial flow
   - Free trial URL.
   - Signup requirements.
   - Trial duration.
   - Onboarding support.
   - Whether demo is also available.
   - Credit card requirement.
   - Expected time to first attribution view.

5. Brand inputs
   - Logo.
   - Color preferences.
   - Existing design system.
   - Competitor references.

## 9. Open Decisions

These decisions can be made after more documentation is uploaded:

- Whether the page should use only English or support bilingual English/Chinese planning.
- Whether the first CTA should be self-serve trial or request trial access.
- Whether to include competitor comparison.
- Whether to name the attribution methodology.
- Whether to lead visually with product screenshot, attribution diagram, or customer result.
- Whether to add a lightweight "built for" use-case section for app growth, ecommerce, lead generation, or multi-market advertisers.

## 10. Success Criteria

The landing page framework is successful if it allows the team to:

- Explain GOC AI Dashboard in one clear narrative.
- Connect the product to advertiser pain quickly.
- Show why unified attribution matters.
- Make upper-funnel measurement understandable.
- Prepare clear placeholders for future uploaded content.
- Support a free trial conversion path.
